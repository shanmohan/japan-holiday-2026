💳 IC Cards — Your Local Transport Pass

How it works: An IC card is a rechargeable tap-and-go transit card (like an Opal card). Load money, tap on at the gate when you enter a station, tap off when you exit — the correct fare is deducted automatically. No need to buy individual tickets!

Where to get them:

  • Adults & Anoshan (13): Download the Welcome Suica app on iPhone before you land, or pick up physical cards at Haneda Airport JR ticket machines.
  • Anoshka (10): Needs a Child IC card (half-price fares). Get this at any JR ticket office — bring her passport as proof of age.
  • 5-year-old: Rides free on all local trains and buses (no card needed).

Works across all 3 cities: The same card works on JR local trains, Metro/subway, city buses, and monorails in Tokyo, Kyoto, and Osaka. Also accepted at convenience stores (7-Eleven, Lawson, FamilyMart), vending machines, and many shops.

How much to load:

  • Tokyo (5 days): ~A$30–40 per adult card
  • Kyoto (4 days): ~A$20–30 per adult card (you’ll use more buses here)
  • Osaka (4 days): ~A$20–30 per adult card
  • A typical train/metro ride costs A$1.50–3.50

Top up anytime: Every station has machines with English. Insert cash or use Apple Pay to reload. Keep at least A$5 on each card to avoid getting stuck at a gate.

Family cost (cards only): A$40 (4 cards × A$10 deposit) • Estimated local transit total: ~A$250–350 for the whole trip
